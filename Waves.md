#### Wave parameters
$$x(t)=A\cdot \cos(2\pi \cdot f\cdot t+\phi)$$
A = amplitude
f = frequency
t = time, or x

## Two basic types
- Transverse
	- Think of a slinky
- Longitudinal
	- Compressed particles

### Important info
($\lambda$) wavelength, The distance from a specific point to the next identical point
(A) amplitude
(T) period (seconds) $T = \frac{1}{f}$
(f) frequency (Hz) $f = \frac{1}{T}$
### Speed of sound
$$v=330+0.6T$$
(T) is temperature in Celsius

### Basic wave equations
$$v=\lambda f = \frac{\lambda}{T}$$
Speed of a wave in a string
$$v=\sqrt{ \frac{F}{m / L} }$$
F is [[Force]] tension (N)
m is [[Mass]] (kg)
L is Length of the string (m)

### Standing wave
A system where certain points remain stationary and others vibrate at maximum amplitude
$$\lambda=\frac{2L}{n}$$
# Sound Intensity
$$I=\frac{P}{A}$$
I is intensity in [[Watt]]/m^2
P is power in [[Watt]]
A is area in m^2
##### Decibels
$$\beta=10*\log\left( \frac{I}{I_{0}} \right)$$
$$I_{0}=1*10^{12}$$
### Fun Facts
- Human ear can hear frequencies from 20 Hz & 20,000 Hz.
- Frequencies below 20 Hz are infrasonic.
- Frequencies above 20,000 Hz are ultrasonic
- Sound travels faster in steel (15x) and water (4x) compared to air.
- Thunder and lightning rule: divide time between seeing lightning and hearing thunder by 5 to get estimated ‘miles away’.
